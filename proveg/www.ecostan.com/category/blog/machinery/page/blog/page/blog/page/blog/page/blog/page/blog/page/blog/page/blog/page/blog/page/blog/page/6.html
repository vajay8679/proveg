ECOSTAN® Pellet Making Machine

Pellet Making Machine




What is a Pellet Machine? A Pellet Machine is used for making pellets from the powdered materials. In other words, it is a type of machine or mill that combines small materials into a huge homogenous mass instead of breaking large materials into smaller pieces. It is also called as ‘Pellet Mill’ or ‘Pellet Press’.

The capacity of Ecostan® Pellet Making Machine scales from 700 to 900 Kg/h (8 mm to 10 mm in diameter).

Benefits:

a) Low maintenance

b) Highly durable

d) Precise operation

e) Easy to operate

f) Best performance

Wood Pelletizing Machine: A wood pelletizing machine is mainly used for wood, straw, peanut shell, bamboo, cotton stalk, rice husk, empty fruit bunch, waste paper or pulverized coal etc.

Cattle Feed Pellet Machine: Cattle Feed pelletizing machine is mainly used for developing pellets of cattle feed depending on the size needed so that it becomes easy to eat for the cattle. Corn, bean pulp, wheat stalk, straw, grass, alfalfa, and rice husk, etc.

What Are The Types Of Pellet Machines?

Pellet Machine can be divided into two categories:

a) Flat Die Pellet Machine: It has simple and convenient structure, adjustable pressure for all different material.

b) Ring Die Pellet Machine: It’s huge output, less power consumption in the crushing of feed is the perfect qualities that flat die could not offer.

How Much Saw Dust Does It Take To Make Pellets?

Generally, little saw dust is used for making pellets. So the first step is to add water to the sawdust till its moisture content is between 8 to 15%. This is considered to the best moisture content for pelletizing. But if you are using wood chips as a raw material, they should be pulverized first. And then put sawdust into the pellet machine slowly.

How Does A Pellet Machine Work?

Large scale Ecostan® pellet mills are normally used to produce wood pellets, animal feed, and fuel pellets for use in a pellet stove.

Pelletizing machine working principle:

Pelletizing machine is one of the biomass molding equipment which is used to make powdered biomass materials into pellets.

a) The starting materials for the pellets production are compressed by the pressing rollers in the matrix.

b) Due to the increased friction and pressure in the matrix, the starting materials gets heated to a high temperature (approximately 60~80 °C).

c) And the materials were compressed into pellets.
